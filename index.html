A lighthouse is 250 m off the coast. Its light rotates at 2 revolutions per minute. How fast does the light beam pass a point on the coastline 40 m from the point directly opposite the lighthouse?

Related Rates: Lighthouse Beam

Geometry

tan θ = x250
x = 250 tan θ

Angular speed

dθdt = 2 rev/min = π15 rad/s

Differentiate with respect to time

dxdt = 250 sec²θ dθdt
sec²θ = 1 + tan²θ = 1 + (x250)²
x 0 m
θ 0.1587 rad
dθ/dt π/15 rad/s
dx/dt 52.4 m/s
At x = 0 m, dxdt ≈ 52.4 m/s. Move to x = 40 m for the asked value.

The beam sweeps faster along the coastline as the point moves farther from the point directly opposite the lighthouse.
